From now until February 26th, residents of Ottawa South who are Canadian citizens and 18 years of age or older can vote for Morgan Gay at the local election office. Be sure to bring ID.

Where to vote: 2210 Bank Street (Beside Winners)

When to vote: From now until February 26th

Monday - Saturday: 10AM - 8PM
Sunday: 12PM - 5PM

You may be presented with a write-in ballot at the local election office. In that case, you will need to write the first and last name of the candidate of your choice (Morgan Gay) on the write-in ballot.

Not sure if you live in Ottawa South? Find information about voting in your electoral district here.

What you need to vote

In order to vote today, you must bring one piece of ID showing both your name and home address to register and receive a ballot. Photo ID is not required.

There are many forms of valid ID, including a driver's license, a credit card statement, a lease, or a utility bill. You can see the full list here.

Closer to the last day to vote, you may receive a voter information card in the mail from Elections Ontario about when and where to vote.

You may bring this voter information card and one piece of ID showing your name to receive a ballot. For example, a health card. See the full list here.

Other ways to vote

You can vote during the advance voting period (February 20, 21, and 22) at your designated advance polling location. Voting location information will be available after February 14th.

You can vote on election day on February 27th. Voting location information will be available after February 14th.

You can apply to vote by mail.

Special Cases

  • Are you studying in Ontario but living away from home for school?
  • Do you not have a permanent address?
  • Do you need to vote while hospitalized?
  • Do you need a home visit from election officials in order to vote?

If these cases apply to you, you can view guidance from Elections Ontario on their FAQ page.

You can also see details about accessible voting in provincial elections.
